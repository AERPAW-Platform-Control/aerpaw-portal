Step 1:

Decide whether you are trying to create a canonical (Program-it-Yourself) experiment, or any other type of experiment. (Refer to the AERPAW User Manual, Section 1.8, if you need more information about these categories.)

Canonical Experiments
In AERPAW, we consider the "canonical" Experiment to be one in which the target resources are AERPAW's AFRNs, APRNs, and programmable vehicles (UAVs, UGVs), and the Experimenter writes code to program the UAV trajectories, and the SDR transmissions, to realize their experimental intent. We also call this the “Program-it-Yourself” (PiY) approach to initiating an experiment. This is the most common experiment type.
Non-Canonical and Custom
AERPAW also supports (i) non-canonical experiment modes, such as Experiment-as-a-Service, Experiment-Development-as-a-Service, Live Limited Access, under the umbrella of General Availability of the platform resources, and also (ii) other custom services such as access to equipment not part of the Generally Available platform, and Bring Your Own Device mode.
